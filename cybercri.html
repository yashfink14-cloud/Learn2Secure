Cyber Crimes: Types, Examples & Prevention

Cybercrime refers to criminal activities carried out using computers, networks, or the internet. These crimes can target individuals, organizations, or even governments. Understanding different types of cybercrimes is the first step toward protecting yourself online.

🔒

Phishing

Phishing involves sending fraudulent messages that appear to come from a reputable source, typically via email, to steal sensitive data like login credentials and credit card numbers.

Prevention Tips

  • Verify sender email addresses
  • Don't click suspicious links
  • Use multi-factor authentication
💰

Online Scams

Online scams trick victims into sending money or revealing personal information through fake offers, lottery winnings, or romance scams.

Prevention Tips

  • Be skeptical of too-good-to-be-true offers
  • Research companies before purchasing
  • Never send money to strangers
🦠

Malware Attacks

Malware is malicious software designed to harm, exploit, or disable computers and computer systems. Types include viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware.

Prevention Tips

  • Install reputable antivirus software
  • Keep systems updated
  • Don't download from untrusted sources
👤

Identity Theft

Identity theft occurs when someone steals your personal information to commit fraud, such as making unauthorized purchases or opening accounts in your name.

Prevention Tips

  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Monitor financial statements regularly
  • Freeze your credit if necessary
💻

Hacking

Hacking involves gaining unauthorized access to data in a system or computer. Cybercriminals may hack to steal information, cause damage, or disrupt services.

Prevention Tips

  • Use strong passwords and 2FA
  • Regularly update software
  • Use firewalls and security tools
😈

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices. It includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, or mean content about someone else.

Prevention Tips

  • Think before you post online
  • Don't respond to bullies
  • Report and block harassers

Real-World Case: 2020 Twitter Bitcoin Scam

In July 2020, hackers gained access to Twitter's internal systems and hijacked high-profile accounts including Barack Obama, Elon Musk, and Bill Gates. The hacked accounts tweeted a Bitcoin scam that promised to double any Bitcoin sent to a specific address. The hackers collected over $100,000 in Bitcoin before Twitter could shut down the scam.

Lesson: Even major companies with significant security resources can be vulnerable to social engineering attacks. This incident highlighted the importance of protecting internal systems and limiting employee access to sensitive controls.

How to Report Cyber Crimes in India

If you believe you've been a victim of cybercrime in India, you can:

  1. File a complaint at your local cyber crime cell
  2. Report online through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
  3. Call the cyber crime helpline: 1930

General Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible
  • Keep your software and operating systems updated
  • Be cautious about what you share online
  • Regularly back up important data
  • Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi networks
  • Educate yourself about current cyber threats
