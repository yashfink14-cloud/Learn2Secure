Cybersecurity: Protecting Yourself and Organizations

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting devices, networks, and data from cyber threats. It includes multiple areas and best practices. Explore the cards below to learn more.

🌐

Network Security

Protects networks from unauthorized access, malware, and data theft using firewalls, VPNs, intrusion detection, and encryption.

Key Measures

  • Firewalls & VPNs
  • Intrusion Detection & Prevention Systems
  • Strong Authentication & Regular Patching
🔐

Data Security

Ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data throughout its lifecycle. Protects personal data, financial info, and intellectual property.

Key Measures

  • Encryption & Access Control
  • Regular Backups & Authentication
  • Employee Security Awareness
📱

Endpoint Security

Secures laptops, phones, servers, and IoT devices from malware, ransomware, and phishing to prevent network breaches.

Best Practices

  • Antivirus & Firewalls
  • Multi-Factor Authentication
  • Patch & Update All Systems
☁️

Cloud Security

Protects cloud-based services and data from breaches, leaks, and theft through encryption, identity management, and secure configuration.

Tips

  • Use strong access controls
  • Enable data encryption at rest & in transit
  • Review cloud security policies regularly
🔗

IoT Security

Safeguards connected devices from unauthorized access and botnets. Example: The Mirai botnet hijacked insecure IoT devices for DDoS attacks.

Why It Matters

  • Protect sensitive data collected by devices
  • Prevent unauthorized remote control
  • Defend critical infrastructure
👥

Operational Security (OpSec)

Focuses on protecting sensitive info through policies, training, and monitoring to prevent leaks or social engineering attacks.

Tips

  • Need-to-Know Access
  • Employee Training on Phishing
  • Monitor Insider Activity
📝

Information Security vs. Information Assurance

Information Security protects all forms of information from modification or destruction. Information Assurance manages risks to ensure data stays confidential and trustworthy.

Case Studies

Colonial Pipeline (2021)

A ransomware attack via a compromised VPN account without MFA. Highlights the need for strong endpoint security and multi-factor authentication.

Equifax Data Breach (2017)

Personal data of 147 million people exposed due to unpatched software. Emphasizes the importance of timely updates and vulnerability management.

Target POS Malware Attack (2013)

Attackers accessed customer credit card data through a third-party HVAC vendor. Shows the need for vendor risk management and network segmentation.

Best Practices for Individuals & Organizations

  • Use strong, unique passwords and a password manager
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication on all accounts
  • Keep all software and devices updated
  • Back up important data regularly
  • Be cautious with links and attachments (phishing awareness)
  • Use VPN on public Wi-Fi
  • Provide regular security training to employees
